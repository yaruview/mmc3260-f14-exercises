HTML links and images

Links

Relative links go to another page within your same site. For example, we can open the previous exercise in a new tab. Typically you do not want to open links in new tabs, but we have to in this example because the other page doesn't have a link to get back here.

Absolute links go to another site. Remember to always validate your HTML early and often to find typos and syntax errors.

Notice the inclusion or exclusion of `http://` in the anchor element's href attribute. The href attribute is required. The target attribute is optional and defaults to opening links in the current tab.

A complete HTML tag is comprised of a one element and any optional attribute/value pairs.

Images
